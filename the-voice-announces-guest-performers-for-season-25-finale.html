The Voice Season 25 is wrapping up next week with a two-night finale. The Top 5 finalists will be taking the stage to earn America’s vote, and the winner will be announced during Tuesday’s live show. The finale will also feature guest performances from several famous names.

The Voice Announces Finale Guest Performers

On Monday night, this season’s Mega Mentor Keith Urban will be performing his song “Messed Up as Me” to kick off the two-night finale, along with the U.S. Army Field Band. Then on Tuesday, guest performers The Black Keys, Jelly Roll, Kate Hudson, Lainey Wilson, Muni Long, and Thomas Rhett will take the stage.

Season 23 winner Gina Miles will also be performing her new single “Wicked Game” during Tuesday’s live results show. The finale will also include appearances from past coaches Kelly Clarkson, CeeLo Green, Niall Horan, Jennifer Hudson, Nick Jonas, Adam Levine, Blake Shelton, and Gwen Stefani to celebrate 25 seasons of the show.

Additionally, the Top 5 contestants will take the stage with their coaches (John Legend, Dan + Shay, Chance the Rapper, and Reba McEntire) to perform duets. Tuesday’s show will end with a winner announcement.

Who Made It to the Top 5?

The Voice Season 25 recently aired its live Semifinals, as the Top 9 was narrowed down to the Top 5. The remaining artists delivered a solo song and a trio performance. Voting was open overnight, and five contestants sang for the Instant Save on Tuesday night.

The Top 5 of this season is Josh Sanders (Team Reba), Nathan Chester (Team Legend), Karen Waldrup (Team Dan+ Shay), Asher HaVon (Team Reba), and Bryan Olesen (Instant Save, Team Legend). That means Chance the Rapper has no artists in the finale this season.
